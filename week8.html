A capacitator stores electricity and then, when there is a drop in electricity, releases it back into the circuit.

Transistors take in a small electrical current and amplify it so a larger current can pass between its collector and
emitter pins.

Resistors reduce the flow of electrical current in the circuit.

LEDs, or light emitting diodes, is a diode that lights up when electricity passes through it.

A diode is a polarized component that only allows electricty to pass through it in one direction.

Breadboards are boards for prototyping electronics. They are covered with a grid of holes, which are split into electrically
continuous rows.

A multimeter is an instrument that measures electricity.

A switch is a component that makes and breaks the electrical flow in a circuit.


AC and DC are two different types of electrical signals.
In AC (alternating current), the direction in which electricity flows is continually reversing.
The rate of reversal is measured in Hertz (the number of reversals per second).

With Direct Current, electricity flows in one direction between power and ground. In this arrangement there is always a positive source of voltage and ground (0V) source of voltage.


Voltage is a quantitative expression of the potential difference in charge between two points in an electrical field.

Current is a flow of electrical charge carriers, usually electrons or electron-deficient atoms.
The common symbol for current is the uppercase letter I.
The standard unit is the ampere, symbolized by A.

Resistance is the opposition that a substance offers to the flow of electric current.
It is represented by the uppercase letter R. The standard unit of resistance is the ohm.
